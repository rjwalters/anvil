\section*{The fire}

Grani has told this story more times than I can count, but I only started
writing it down the September I turned the recorder on. ``Well, I remember
it clear as anything,'' she said, settling into the porch chair she always
takes. ``It was a Tuesday, hot as blazes,'' and Pa had come running up the
road hollering that the mill was on fire.

She was eight years old the day the factory burned. Her little brother
Thomas was still a baby.

\section*{The letter}

Six weeks after the family left Carthage, Ruth wrote from the road. The
journey west had been long but safe, she said, and she expected to reach
Tulsa by the end of the month.

Whether she made it on schedule, none of us can say for certain — the
next letter in the box is dated three months later, from Tulsa itself,
with no word of the arrival in between.
