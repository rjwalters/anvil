# Investment memo: Halcyon Instruments

## Recommendation

Pass on the Series A at the proposed terms. Revisit if the company closes
either of the two reference customers it named in diligence, or if the
round reprices below $40M post.

## Company summary

Halcyon builds benchtop spectrometers for process chemists. The founding
team spun out of a national lab in 2023; the CTO holds the core patents on
the cavity design, and the CEO ran product at a mid-size analytical
instruments firm for six years. Headcount is fourteen, of whom nine are
technical.

The product replaces a $250K instrument with a $60K one for a specific
class of reaction monitoring. Customers we spoke with confirmed the
performance claim on narrow-band measurements but flagged calibration
drift on longer runs. The company says the drift is firmware-correctable
and has committed to a fix in the next release.

## Why pass now

Three reasons drive the recommendation.

First, the pipeline is thinner than the deck suggests. Of the eleven
"active evaluations" listed, four are unpaid pilots at academic labs and
two have been stalled for over a quarter. Paid commercial engagements
number three, with a combined contract value of $410K.

Second, the proposed valuation prices in a platform story the data does
not yet support. Revenue is instrument sales plus a small service line.
The recurring-revenue narrative depends on a consumables program that has
not shipped and has no committed customers.

Third, the competitive window may be shorter than management believes.
Two incumbent vendors have announced lower-cost lines for 2027. Neither
matches Halcyon's sensitivity today, but both have distribution that
Halcyon must build from scratch.

## What would change our minds

A signed order from either named reference customer would address the
pipeline concern directly. So would a consumables pilot with usage data
from at least one commercial site. On valuation, a price below $40M post
would compensate for the platform risk we cannot underwrite today.

We recommend keeping the relationship warm. The team is strong, the
instrument is real, and the category is worth owning if the commercial
evidence catches up to the engineering.
